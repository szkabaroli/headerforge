# Privacy Policy — HeaderForge

Last updated: 2026-07-18

HeaderForge collects no data, sends no data, and has no servers. Everything you
configure stays on your computer.

## What is stored

Only the configuration you create in the popup: your profiles, their names, URL
filters, and the header names and values you enter. It is saved with the
browser's `chrome.storage.local` on your device and never sent anywhere. There
is no analytics, tracking, or remote code, and all logic ships inside the
extension package.

## Access to your browsing

HeaderForge requests the `declarativeNetRequest` permission and host access
(`<all_urls>`) so the browser can apply your header rules to matching requests.
The browser performs the header changes itself. The extension cannot read or
receive your requests, responses, cookies, or page content; it only tells the
browser which headers to set or remove.

## Your control

Your configuration lives only in local storage. Removing a profile or clicking
Clear deletes it immediately, and uninstalling the extension removes all of it.
